Buldhana Tourism Guide

“Where Heritage Meets Nature”

Geography and Location

  • Buldhana is located in central Maharashtra, approximately 130 km from the state capital, Nagpur.
  • The region is marked by diverse topography, including hills, rivers, and plains. It is part of the Deccan Plateau, with the Penganga River flowing through the district, adding to its scenic beauty.
  • The climate of Buldhana is typically tropical, with hot summers, a monsoon season, and mild winters. The region receives moderate rainfall during the monsoon, making it lush and green during that period

Tourist Attractions

  • Lonar Lake: One of the few hyper-velocity impact craters in the world, Lonar Lake is a major tourist attraction. It is not only scientifically significant but also features temples around its perimeter, making it an important site for both nature lovers and devotees.
  • Shegaon: Known for the famous Gajanan Maharaj Temple, Shegaon is a pilgrimage destination for thousands of devotees each year. The town is also known for its clean and well-planned surroundings.
  • Purna Wildlife Sanctuary: A haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts, this sanctuary is home to various species of flora and fauna. It is a great place for trekking and exploring the region's natural beauty.

History and Culture

  • Buldhana has a rich historical and cultural legacy, influenced by the Maratha Empire and various other dynasties that ruled the region.
  • The region boasts ancient temples, forts, and monuments that reflect its rich cultural past, which attracts both history enthusiasts and pilgrims.
  • Buldhana is known for its traditional festivals, including Makar Sankranti, Ganesh Chaturthi, and Navratri, which are celebrated with zeal and enthusiasm.

Local Cuisine and Economy

  • Buldhana offers a rich array of Maharashtrian cuisine, with dishes like Vada Pav, Poha, and Puran Poli being local favorites. The food here reflects the simplicity and rich flavors of rural Maharashtra.
  • Agriculture is the backbone of Buldhana’s economy, with crops like cotton, soybean, and paddy being cultivated. The region also has a growing agro-based industry.
  • The district is also known for its handicrafts, such as cotton textiles and pottery.

Historical Attractions

  • Lonar Lake:A unique hyper-velocity impact crater, Lonar Lake is a geological wonder and features ancient temples around its perimeter.
  • Buldhana Fort:A historical site offering insights into the region’s past, with remnants of old structures and beautiful views of the surrounding area.

Visit Religious & Spiritual Sites

  • Gajanan Maharaj Temple (Shegaon):A revered pilgrimage destination, the Gajanan Maharaj Temple in Shegaon attracts thousands of devotees every year.
  • Gajanan Maharaj Temple (Pimpalgaon):Another important pilgrimage site dedicated to Gajanan Maharaj, located in Pimpalgaon.
  • Lonar Lake Temples:The lake is surrounded by several ancient temples, some of which are dedicated to Lord Shiva, Vishnu, and other deities.

Nature & Adventure

  • Rajur Ghat Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Lonar Crater Lake.
  • Dnyanganga Wildlife Sanctuary.

Food & Shopping

  • Shegaon Kachori – Famous crispy snack found in Shegaon.
  • Local Maharashtrian Food – Try Misal Pav, Pithla Bhakri, and Kharwas.
  • Handicrafts & Souvenirs – Visit Shegaon and Sindkhed Raja for local artifacts.

Offbeat & Hidden Gems

  • Gandhari Waterfall (seasonal) – A hidden gem near the Buldhana region.
  • Dhotra Lake – Less crowded, perfect for a peaceful outing.
  • Malkapur Hills – Great for short hikes and scenic drives.

General Safety

  • Summers (March–June) are extremely hot, with temperatures reaching 45°C. Carry light cotton clothes, sunscreen, sunglasses, and stay hydrated.
  • Monsoons (July–September) can cause waterlogging in some areas. Check weather updates before traveling
  • Winters (November–February) are pleasant, but mornings and nights can be slightly chilly, so carry light woolens.

Health Precautions

  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid waterborne diseases.
  • Be cautious of street food hygiene; choose clean, well-reviewed places for local delicacies
  • Wear sunscreen

Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly at temples
  • Ask before taking photos
  • Respect local customs

Travel & Transportation Safety

  • Drive cautiously as rural roads can have potholes.
  • Avoid night travel on highways due to limited lighting
  • Auto-rickshaws & buses are available, but confirm fares in advance.
